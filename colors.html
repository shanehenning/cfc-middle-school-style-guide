Grade-Level Colors

Each grade level has a unique color used to identify it. When used as a background color, most of the grade colors take white type. When reversing type on a field of Grade 2 or Grade 8 yellow, gray is used, typically 85% black. Tints of gray are generally preferred over grade-color tints for color fields in tables or text boxes. A 9–12% tint of black seems to print best. Exact tints should be vetted with each printing. When grade-color tints are employed, we use a 10–15% tint, though percentage varies depending on the printing method.

Program & Unit Colors

The Bullying Prevention Unit (BPU), Child Protection Unit (CPU), and Second Step program also have their own specific colors that should be used with associated projects. Second Step blue is also used in Committee for Children executive, advocacy, and marketing materials.

Neutrals may need adjustment depending on printer.
